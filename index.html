Sunday meal prep — 2h
Goal: prepare the bases that let you assemble meals in <15 min all week. Everything keeps 4–5 days in the fridge.
1 Oven at 200°C — Roasted vegetables (45 min)

Use two trays side by side:

  • Tray 1 — Carrots (6–8, whole or halved): peel, toss with olive oil + salt. These go into the carrot-ginger soup (step 4) and as toppings for the grain bowls (Tuesday + Saturday).
  • Tray 2 — Beets (4, quartered) + Celeriac (½, 2cm cubes): peel, toss with olive oil + salt. Beets go into the warm beet & lentil salad (Saturday lunch). Celeriac goes into the grain bowls.

Roast both trays 40–45 min until caramelised. Let cool, store in separate containers.

2 Farro (30 min, while the oven runs)

Rinse 300g farro. Cook in generously salted boiling water for 25–30 min until tender but still chewy — taste to check. Drain well, spread on a tray to cool (prevents clumping). Store in a container.

Used in: Grain bowl with tahini-tamari dressing (Tuesday dinner + Wednesday lunch) · Warm beet & farro salad (Saturday lunch).

3 Green lentils (25 min, while the oven runs)

Rinse 400g green lentils du Puy. Cover with cold water (2× their volume) + 1 onion halved + 1 bay leaf + 2 thyme sprigs. Bring to a boil, then simmer 20–25 min. They must hold their shape — stop cooking when just tender. Drain, discard aromatics, season with salt. Cool and store.

Used in: Grain bowl (Tuesday dinner + Wednesday lunch) · Warm beet & lentil salad (Saturday lunch) · Dal (Thursday dinner — uses a separate 400g batch cooked fresh that evening, these are for the bowls and salad).

4 Caramelized onions (45 min, low heat)

Thinly slice 8 large yellow onions. Heat 3 tbsp olive oil + 30g butter in a wide pan over the lowest possible heat. Add onions + a pinch of salt. Cook 40–45 min, stirring every 5–10 min, until deep golden brown and jammy. Do not rush with high heat — they'll turn bitter.

Used in: Pasta with caramelized onions, white miso & Gruyère (Monday dinner + Tuesday lunch).

5 Carrot-ginger soup (15 min, once carrots are roasted)

Take all the roasted carrots from step 1. Blend with 700ml hot vegetable stock + 3–4cm fresh ginger, grated. Once smooth, add 200ml coconut milk and blend again until very silky. Off heat, add juice of 1 lemon gradually, tasting as you go. Season with salt and pepper. Pour into a jar or container.

Used as: Starter C — served warm with fresh coriander + a drizzle of olive oil (Saturday lunch · Sunday lunch). Reheat gently, thin with a splash of stock if too thick.

6 Miso soup base (10 min)

Soak 30g dried wakame in cold water for 5 min, drain and roughly chop. Bring 1.5L light vegetable stock to a gentle simmer (not a boil). Add 2 leeks, thinly sliced + the wakame. Simmer 5 min. Let cool, store in a pot — do not add miso yet.

When serving: reheat to ~70°C (hot but not boiling). Add 50g silken tofu per person, cut in cubes. Dissolve 1 tbsp white miso per person in a ladle of hot broth, then stir into the pot. Never boil after adding miso — the heat destroys the live bacteria.

Used as: Starter A — Monday · Wednesday · Friday dinners · Saturday dinner · Sunday dinner.

What's ready: roasted carrots · roasted beets + celeriac · cooked farro · cooked lentils · caramelized onions · carrot-ginger soup · miso soup base — the whole week assembles in under 15 min per meal.
